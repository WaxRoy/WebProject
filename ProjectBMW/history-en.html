Establishment (1916)

BMW was founded in 1916 in Munich, Germany, under the name "Rapp Motorenwerke". It initially
operated as a company producing aircraft engines.

The Foundation of the BMW Brand (1917)

After Rapp Motorenwerke experienced financial problems, a group of entrepreneurs led by Franz
Josef Popp took over the company's assets and re-established the company as "Bayerische Motoren Werke".
This is considered the moment when the BMW brand as it is known today was born.

Aircraft Engines and Motorcycle Production (1920s)

BMW developed itself especially in the production of aircraft engines and motorcycles in the
1920s. Their success in motorcycle production has helped BMW reach a wide customer base.

Transition to Automobile Production (1930s)

By the 1930s, BMW also focused on automobile production. The famous "328" model symbolized
the brand's success in automobile production.

War Period (1939-1945)

During World War II, BMW concentrated on the production of military equipment and aircraft
engines to focus on the war effort.

Post-War Period (1945-1960s)

Following the war, BMW's Munich facilities were severely damaged by allied bombings. However,
the company rebounded successfully in the 1950s and 1960s, particularly with the "Neue Klasse"
(New Class) line of cars.

Focus on Performance and Luxury Segment (1970s)

BMW attracted attention with its performance and luxury-oriented cars in the 1970s. Models such
as the "3 Series" and "5 Series" positioned the brand as a premium automaker.

Motorcycle and Automobile Portfolio (1980s - Present)

Today, BMW has a wide portfolio of motorcycles and automobiles. It is a globally recognized brand
with its products in various segments such as performance models, luxury sedans, SUVs and electric vehicles.

BMW has a respected position in the automotive industry with its innovative approach to design and
engineering, driving pleasure-oriented models and strong brand identity.
